On the iPhone Naming

Sep 4th, 2012

There has been a lot of debate on the new iPhone naming. should it be named iPhone 5 or follow the path of other Apple products and drop the name?.

Some people say that the 5 suffix is wrong, therefore it’s going to be named new iPhone others even say it should be iPhone 6. These people are wrong, here is why.

Marketing perspective

Yes, it holds NO logic, unless you look at it under another perspective, the marketing perspective.

The first iPhone launched without a suffix because other than being an iPhone (which on itself it’s great) on the cellular technical side was an ordinary phone.

The iPhone 3G featured a faster network, hence the name.

iPhone 3GS not only has a faster network, but a substantial improvement on the graphical/computation part enough to sell it. The S, chosen from speed is a good fit, don’t you think?.

iPhone 4, the retina display and the speed improvement it’s cool, a true revolution on the iPhone but calling it iPhone 3GSRS (Retina, Speed) is not a good name. So, let’s see, it was the device fourth generation and iPhone 4, sounds good to me. BOOM.

iPhone 4S, to follow the same naming after the 3G/3GS was easy, already out there and if the speed improvement wasn’t enough it had Siri to sell the S.

Now tell me, after having a lot of people calling the new iPhone, iPhone 5 and the iPhone 3G/3GS looked as a single iteration, the 5 sticks really great, even though it’s going to be the sixth iPhone to sell, so yeah, marketing-wise works.

Technical perspective

On the other hand, we have the perspective of those who actually make the thing, let’s see.

The X stands for the variation of each iteration (Network stuff)

iPhone 1,1: The first iteration, logical call. There were no variations.

iPhone 1,2: Say what?. Well, you see, this iPhone just had a cellular chip changed and the back was esthetic, dosn’t count (Technically).

iPhone 2,1: Hey, we changed the cellular chip, improved the GPU and CPU and a bunch of other things. It’s a new beast.

iPhone 3,x: We doubled the pixels and made it faster, and engineered a new antenna, we deserve a star.

iPhone 4,x: This Siri thing is really great, a software engineer marvel, besides, it’s faster.

iPhone 5,x: We enlarged the screen, we had to make a lot of changes to keep it running smoothly, and maybe, fasterâ€¦

So…

As you can see, the 5 makes a pretty good case on both sides of the coin, it may be the one. On the other hand we have the iPad precedent that got rid off the number, the new iPhone it’s also a compelling name from the marketing standpoint.

Personally, I prefer dropping the number so this confusion ends, now we can only wait.
